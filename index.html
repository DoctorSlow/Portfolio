About

Greetings! My name is Jeremy Joseph Lee. I'm passionate about education, music, coding, and facilitating fun and knowledge--preferably at the same time. I have a BA in anthropology and East Asian studies from the University at Albany - SUNY and an MA in applied linguistics from the University of Montana. I currently teach English as a Second Language (ESL) to adult learners at the University of Arizona here in Tucson. I've also trained English teachers in the US, China, and Mexico.

I love facilitating learning, and I extend that love to my hobby as a radio DJ. As my alter ego DoctorSlow, I host two weekly drive-time shows on 99.1FM // Downtown Radio: The Waiting Room (Thursdays 7-9am) and The Tonic (Mondays 5-7pm). For each of these shows, I curate a live musical "mixtape" comprised of new, old, local, and international "left-of-the-dial" rock'n'roll. The shows can be heard over the airwaves in Tucson or streamed worldwide from the website or our mobile app: Downtown Radio Tucson.

Working as a full-stack developer, I am able to connect these worlds. My patience and knowledge as an educator enable me to bridge the gap between front-end users or clients and the often esoteric nature of web design and development. I hope to continue channeling these dual passions through future work with musicians, record labels, or radio stations.

Hangman Game

This is a basic variation on the classic hangman type game. A random word is chosen for the player and represented by underscores on the screen. You must guess the letters or 'die' trying. Each correct guess will reveal the letter(s) in the puzzle, while each incorrect guess will land you one step closer to the edge as your incorrect guesses are limited. Good luck.

Dice Game

This game is a basic matching and adding game similar to blackjack. You have four dice with hidden values. You must click on each die to reveal it's value and add it to your sum. The goal is to match your dice sum with the computer-generated random target number. If you do that, you win. Otherwise...
